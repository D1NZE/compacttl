Crystal Leaderboard

How the leaderboard works

Players are sorted by their latest tracked Crystal result. Staff results from /close, high-test results from /highclose, and migrations from /miragrate update the player tier automatically.

Each tier column groups high and low placements from HT1/LT1 down to HT5/LT5. Click a player row to view their Discord ID, current tier, and region.

Regions are kept separate for queue organization, but placements stay on the same Crystal board. If a player retests or receives a higher result, the newest staff-submitted result replaces the older one.

HT results show stronger performance inside that tier number, while LT results show the lower side of the same tier number. Movement is based on testing results, not manual leaderboard edits.

Tier Information

How tiers work

Overall ranks are ordered from HT1 as the highest placement down through LT5. HT means high tier and LT means low tier inside the same number group, so HT3 is above LT3 and LT3 is above HT4.

Players update when staff closes a test, closes a high-test ticket, or migrates a result from another tierlist. Search can find players by Minecraft name, Discord ID, tier, region, or result source.

A normal test can place or update a player in the tracked tiers. High tests are for players who already qualify for higher movement, starting from LT3+ and continuing toward HT1.

Migrated results keep the player visible on the same leaderboard while showing that the placement came from another server or tierlist source. Retests can overwrite migrated placements when staff records a new Vanilla-compact result.
